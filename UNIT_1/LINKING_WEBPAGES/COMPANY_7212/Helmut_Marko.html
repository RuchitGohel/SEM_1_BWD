Oracle Red Bull Racing




Helmut Marko

Marko was born in Graz, Austria. He was a school friend of Jochen Rindt, who became Formula One world champion in 1970.
Marko competed in several race series, including 10 Formula One Grands Prix in 1971 and 1972, but scored no World Championship points.
He had more success in endurance racing, winning the 1971 24 Hours of Le Mans, driving a Martini-Porsche 917K with Gijs van Lennep.
During that year,they set a distance record which remained unbeaten until the 2010 24 Hours of Le Mans (5,335.313 km, at an average of
222.304 km/h - changes to the track reduced the average speed.)
